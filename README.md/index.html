The Problem

Difficulty in identifying the raw materials located in the depths of the ocean and in gathering environmental data at various depths.

Problem Analysis

Humans have only explored about 20% of the ocean‚Äôs depths! There is a high likelihood of undiscovered ecosystems and large quantities of raw materials we have yet to fully understand. These materials lie beneath this vast expanse, and in the future, we may develop machines to extract them‚Äînothing is impossible!

The Solution

  • The robot uses sensors to measure pressure, oxygen levels (excluding the oxygen within the water itself), and other parameters at different depths.
  • It employs wavelengths to detect raw materials (like iron) through each material‚Äôs spectral signature, which is the unique pattern of light absorption or emission by a particular material across different wavelengths.

Why Our Solution?

  • Biomimicry in Design: Inspired by marine creatures, resembling a fish for better maneuverability.
  • Renewable Energy: Harnesses energy from wave motion or sunlight.
  • Low Cost: Economical compared to submarines and other large vehicles.
  • Eco-Friendly Materials: Made of lightweight, high-pressure-resistant, and environmentally friendly materials.

How Does This Robot Help Our World?

  • Supporting Scientific Research: Provides valuable data for scientists, sparking further studies.
  • Enhancing Educational Curricula: Inspires students and leads to innovative ideas for resource extraction.

Summary

Our robot tackles challenges in exploring deep-sea ecosystems. Using sensors to measure salinity, temperature, pH, and other environmental data, it enables better understanding and potential utilization of oceanic resources.
